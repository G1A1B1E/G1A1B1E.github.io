How it all connects.

Today we have things like 500,000 MHz (5G) broadband for our cell phones and fiber optic cables. We use these things for TV and the internet. Well how does it work I’ll tell you, there are underground wires that connect to the service provider’s station and into your homes. The cables enter your home and connect’s to something called a modem, the modem then connects to the router which creates invisible waves that when connected to your device produce Wireless internet (WIFI). There is another cable that connects to your modem and then to your TV to show the channels. Where does everything gets its “magic” It comes from the serves where the cables come from to connect to the internet. When you search something up it sends signals through those wires to the servers which gives you your web page or search.

Waste

E-waste represents 2% of Canada’s trash in landfills, but it equals 70% of overall toxic waste. 20 to 50 million metric tons of e-waste are disposed worldwide every year. Cell phones and other electronic items contain high amounts of precious metals like gold or silver.
